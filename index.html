Welcome to Majji Research Group at Ohio University

We work at the intersection of electrochemical engineering and multiphase flow

About Our Research

Transitioning towards a sustainable future requires solving monumental challenges including access to renewable energy and clean water and reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Addressing these global challenges requires significant advancement of large scale electrochemical architectures in the fields of energy storage, water treatment, and electrification of chemical manufacturing.

Graduate Students

  • Jeyaseelan Krishna Udiyappan
    I am Jeyaseelan from India, currently pursuing my PhD in Chemical Engineering at Ohio University. I hold an undergraduate degree in Electrochemical Engineering from the Central Electrochemical Research Institute, India. My research focuses on exploring the rheology of complex fluids and their applications in electrochemical flow systems through both experimental studies and modeling. Beyond my academic work, I enjoy running and biking.
  • Michael Gumoshabe
    I am Michael Gumoshabe and I am from Uganda. I am a PhD Candidate in Mechanical Engineering. My research focuses on multiphase flow systems in oil and gas pipelines, with a particular emphasis on understanding the complex interactions that occur between gas, liquid, and sometimes solid phases during transportation. Specifically, I study flow pattern regimes, analyzing how different regimes form under varying operating conditions and how they influence the efficiency and stability of fluid transportation. Beyond flow, I am also investigating the implications of these regimes on internal pipeline corrosion, seeking to establish the mechanisms through which flow conditions accelerate or mitigate material degradation. The ultimate goal of my research is to contribute to the development of predictive models and mitigation strategies that can improve flow assurance, extend pipeline integrity, and enhance the safe and sustainable operation of oil and gas transportation systems. Outside research, I enjoy football, music and travelling.
  • Favour Bawa
    Favour Bawa is a Ph.D. student and Research Assistant at the Institute for Corrosion and Multiphase Technology (ICMT) at Ohio University. Her research focuses on enhancing the resilience of CO2 transmission pipelines, specifically modeling multiphase flow dynamics and corrosion mechanisms within the CCT Joint Industry Project. With a strong background in Python programming, Visual Basic .NET, and frontend development, Favour merges her technical expertise with a passion for advancing sustainable energy systems. When she’s not immersed in research or coding, you can find her mentoring, building, and occasionally laughing at memes that only engineers seem to appreciate.
  • Abdallah Wusu-Gim Ali
    Abdallah Wusu-Gim Ali is a master’s student in Chemical Engineering at Ohio University and a member of the Majji Research Group. He is from Ghana, West Africa, and earned a BSc in Petroleum Engineering from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST) in November 2023. After graduation, he worked as an Operations Engineer at the Ghana National Gas Company. His research focuses on modeling multiphase flow systems to evaluate and predict their impact on corrosion in the oil and gas transport pipelines. Outside of academics, Abdallah enjoys watching football and is a devoted supporter of Liverpool FC.
